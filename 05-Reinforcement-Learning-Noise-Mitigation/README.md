# 05-Reinforcement-Learning-Noise-Mitigation

Quantum Reinforcement Learning (QRL) research repository for studying policy learning stability under realistic NISQ noise and error mitigation.

## Research Objective

Train a quantum policy to control a navigation agent that transitions:

- `Searching (state=0)` -> `Target Found (state=1)`

Benchmark and compare learning performance in:

1. Ideal quantum simulation
2. Noisy quantum simulation (IBM-like noise model)
3. Noise-mitigated execution (TREX + ZNE, resilience-level-2 style)

## Quantum Policy Design

- Policy circuit uses:
  - Angle embedding (`RX`, `RY`) for classical state encoding
  - `RealAmplitudes` (or `EfficientSU2`) ansatz
  - `num_qubits=2`, `reps=2`, `entanglement="full"`
- Action logits are expectation values of single-qubit Pauli-Z observables.
- Action distribution is computed with softmax.

## Learning Algorithm

- Algorithm: REINFORCE policy gradient
- Loss: `L(theta) = -sum_t G_t * log(pi_theta(a_t|s_t))`
- Optimizer: Adam

### Parameter-Shift Rule (Exact Quantum Gradients)

For variational gates generated by Pauli operators, gradients are analytically exact:

`d f(theta) / d theta = [f(theta + pi/2) - f(theta - pi/2)] / 2`

This avoids finite-difference bias and gives stable policy-gradient updates for PQCs.

## Noise and Mitigation

### Noise Model

- IBM-style noise from `ibm_osaka` fake backend (via Qiskit fake provider)
- Includes:
  - Gate errors
  - Readout errors
  - Decoherence channels

### Mitigation (Resilience Level 2 Concept)

- TREX-style correction:
  - Corrects expectation-value bias from symmetric readout confusion.
- ZNE:
  - Scales effective noise by circuit folding
  - Measures observables at multiple noise scales
  - Extrapolates to zero-noise estimate

## Why Quantum Policy Networks

PQC policies can represent control policies with compact parameterizations. In low-dimensional control/navigation tasks, they may reach competitive behavior using fewer trainable parameters than typical classical neural policy networks, which is attractive for sample-efficient robotic control loops.

## Applications

- Robot navigation under uncertain sensing
- Autonomous control with hardware-aware optimization
- Quantum-safe robotics pipelines where quantum control policies are validated under realistic noise
